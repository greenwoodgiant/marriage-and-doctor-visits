States that wait to get Married don't wait to see the Doctor (and vice versa)

---

Oddly enough, there is a very strong correlation between the median age at which people get married and the rate at which people have gone 5 or more years since their last doctor's visit.

Age used is average of Male and Female Median Ages at First Marriage for the year 2014.

For the percentage, respondents were asked how long it had been since their last doctor's visit, and given the options 'under 1 year', 'under 2 years', 'under 5 years', '5 years or more', and 'Never been'. Percentage shown is the number of "5+ years" and "Never" responses compared to the total number of responses for each state.)

There are three fairly distinct groupings of states: High Age of Marriage / Low Rate of 5+ Years, vice versa, and the middle ground.

The first group is comprised of New England and Puerto Rico, where the youngest median age is just shy of 30 at 29.5 (Maryland), and the highest rate is a hair over 5% (New Jersey). States where people are waiting until their 30's to get hitched are far more likely to have seen a doctor within the last 5 years.

On the other side of the graph are mostly Rural and/or Midwestern states, where ages range from 25-27 and rates are all over 10%.

The remaining states hover between 5-10% response rate and between 26-29 for the first marriage, but remove only a handful of outliers, and there is a fairly clear trendline that slopes down in age as the response percent rises.
